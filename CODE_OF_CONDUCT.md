# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

DeepLaw contributors and maintainers commit to a respectful, professional, and
harassment-free community. Participation is welcome regardless of experience,
background, identity, nationality, disability, appearance, or personal belief.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas and evidence rather than people.
- State uncertainty and distinguish implementation facts from legal opinions.
- Respect privacy, licensing, confidentiality, and coordinated disclosure.
- Accept maintainer decisions about project scope and safety boundaries.
- Correct mistakes without hiding relevant evidence or blaming reporters.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, insults, or unwanted sexual attention;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- deliberate disruption, spam, impersonation, or deceptive claims;
- pressuring anyone to reveal case data, credentials, private legal sources, or
  confidential vulnerability details;
- presenting DeepLaw output or project participation as legal representation,
  legal advice, a factual finding, or an adjudicative conclusion;
- knowingly submitting material that the contributor may not redistribute.

## Project-specific data boundary

Community channels, issues, pull requests, examples, and tests must not contain
case-private data, credentials, private identifiers, source DOCX/PDF files,
generated release databases, OCR corpora, or restricted legal text. Use
synthetic or source-free examples.

DeepLaw code is distributed under Apache License 2.0. Legal-source packages are
not distributed with the repository and remain subject to their own provenance,
privacy, and licensing controls.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
and other project spaces, as well as when someone officially represents the
project in public.

## Reporting and enforcement

Do not report conduct incidents by publishing a victim's identity or sensitive
details in a public issue. For abuse occurring on GitHub, use GitHub's private
[Report Abuse](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse) channel. If a
maintainer has already provided you a private contact channel, you may use it;
otherwise request a private channel without disclosing the incident details.

Security vulnerabilities have a separate private process in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and must not be reported as conduct issues or public
bugs.

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or
permanently exclude participants whose conduct violates this policy. Reports
will be handled as privately and fairly as the available platform tools allow.
